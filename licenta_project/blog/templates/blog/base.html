Seasonal Gardening Guide
Spring (March - May)
  • Start seedlings indoors for warm-season flowers
  • Plant cool-season annuals like pansies and snapdragons
  • Prepare soil by adding compost and organic matter
  • Begin regular watering schedule as temperatures rise
  • Prune winter-damaged branches and dead-head spent blooms
  • Apply balanced fertilizer to established perennials
Summer (June - August)
  • Water early morning or evening to reduce evaporation
  • Apply mulch around plants to retain moisture
  • Provide shade for sensitive flowers during heat waves
  • Regular deadheading to encourage continuous blooming
  • Monitor for pests and diseases, treat as needed
  • Harvest flowers for bouquets and dried arrangements
Autumn (September - November)
  • Plant spring-blooming bulbs like tulips and daffodils
  • Collect seeds from favorite annual flowers
  • Reduce watering as temperatures cool down
  • Add compost and fallen leaves to garden beds
  • Cut back perennials after first frost
  • Protect tender plants with mulch or covers
Winter (December - February)
  • Plan next year's garden layout and order seeds
  • Care for indoor flowering plants and herbs
  • Maintain tools and equipment for spring readiness
  • Study plant catalogs and research new varieties
  • Create garden journal with notes from past year
  • Enjoy forced bulbs and winter-blooming houseplants

Remember that timing may vary based on your specific climate zone and local weather conditions.
